Launch windows — Hohmann opportunities

Each window is a moment when departing on a Hohmann transfer arc will place you near the destination at arrival. The Δv figures are computed from mean orbital radii and are therefore constant per body pair — a real porkchop plot would show per-window variation. Only meaningful for two planets.

Three craft now beyond the planets, reckoned from their last known heliocentric range and outward speed — distance from Earth is approximated as distance from the Sun, accurate to within about one astronomical unit.

What's overhead, right now

Live ADS-B positions of aircraft near a place you choose, from transponder broadcasts picked up by a global network of ground receivers — typically a few seconds old, not a satellite image. Coverage is good along flight corridors and near airports, and often empty over quiet areas; that is the real sky, not a fault of this page.

Spread across the search radius — click a band to jump there
Refreshing automatically every 8 seconds at whichever radius is selected above. Altitude in feet (barometric, with GNSS-geometric alongside when reported), speed in knots, heading in degrees from true north, range from the searched point; the expanded detail panel adds km/h, mph, metres, m/s, and Mach alongside these. Tap any aircraft to expand its full reported telemetry, including a photo where one is on file. The line below each aircraft in its collapsed state shows climb/descent rate and route; how the position was derived — ADS-B is a direct GPS report from the aircraft and is highly accurate; MLAT is computed from signal-arrival-time differences between ground receivers and can be noticeably less precise; older "seen" values mean the position is correspondingly stale — is shown once expanded.
All aircraft with a reported position are shown, sorted by distance from the searched point. Aircraft broadcasting without a position (Mode S only) are counted in the status but cannot be placed on a distance ranking and are not shown. Two nearby places with a wide radius selected will often show the same aircraft, since their search areas overlap — narrow the radius to tell them apart.
Operator name and full aircraft type are looked up from the callsign prefix and ICAO type code against a curated list of common carriers and aircraft — not every operator is included, and an unrecognised one simply shows its raw code instead of a name. Origin and destination, when shown, come from a separate public flight-route database keyed to the callsign; this data is volunteer-maintained and occasionally stale or wrong, and only exists at all for flights with a published schedule, so private, general-aviation, and most military aircraft will correctly show no route rather than a guessed one. Aircraft photos, when shown, are crowd-submitted stock images of that registration or airframe type — not a live picture of this specific flight overhead. Mach number is taken directly from the feed when the aircraft reports it; otherwise it is estimated from true airspeed and a standard-atmosphere model of the local speed of sound at the reported altitude, marked "est." accordingly. Passenger information is never public for any aircraft, anywhere, and is not something this page could show even in principle.

The full table

Sun and the eight planets, in millions of kilometres. Touch any figure to set it above.

Positions are reckoned from VSOP87 truncated series for the eight planets, and from mean orbital elements propagated circularly for Ceres, Pluto, and all moons — a method in the tradition of the old nautical almanacs, accurate to a few percent of true range for the planets and somewhat less so for the dwarf bodies. Moons are placed at their mean distance from their parent planet, with an orbital phase set once per visit for variety rather than drawn from a true epoch.

Light is taken to travel at 299,792.458 kilometres the second. The historical figures beneath the reckoning are offered for scale, not as relevant comparisons of date.
